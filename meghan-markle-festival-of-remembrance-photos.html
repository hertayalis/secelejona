Meghan Markle Wears a Black Dress at the Festival of Remembrance

July 10, 2024
blog

Meghan Markle and Prince Harry stepped out in London on Saturday night for the Royal British Legion Festival of Remembrance at the Royal Albert Hall.

The annual concert commemorates the people who tragically lost their lives in conflicts. Per the British Legion's official website, 2019 is significant as it marks the "75th anniversaries of the great battles of 1944 and the collaboration and friendship of the British, Commonwealth, and Allied armies who fought them."

For the concert, Duchess Meghan wore a black brocade dress with a cinched belted waist, a low-cut neckline, and a flared skirt. Per Twitter user @WhatMeghanWore, the duchess's dress was custom Erdem. She accessorized her look with her beloved Aquazzura Deneuve bow pointy toe pumps, and a gorgeous pair of Jessica McCormack ruby heart earrings.

This year's commemorative concert featured performances from pop stars including Leona Lewis and James Blunt, and was attended by several members of the royal family, such as Queen Elizabeth, Prince Andrew, and the Princess Royal.

A royal source previously told BAZAAR.com that Meghan and Harry will spend Thanksgiving in America this year, before returning to the United Kingdom for Christmas with the royal family. The Duke and Duchess of Sussex are reportedly looking forward to having an extended break over the festive season.
